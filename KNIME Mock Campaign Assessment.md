# Campaign Assessment

## What’s Missing

* That $52k total spend figure…is some of this on channels outside of the 3 in the table? Because the numbers don’t really add up.  
  * It’s difficult to draw reliable conclusions without full spend reconciliation, but I proceeded assuming that the impression numbers should be higher.  
* I see the 30% cumulative number for demos from target accounts, but I would be curious to see what that percentage is for each channel individually.  
* SALs, Conv Value, etc.  
  * Adequate detail is given to conclude that we’d definitely like for the campaign to perform better, but knowing if we did manage to get some SALs out of it  
* Are MQL weights channel-agnostic?  
  * For this exercise’s purposes, I’ve proceeded assuming they are for now.

## Performance Takeaways

Overall, these numbers look like they came from a primary objective of cost per MQL, but the campaign brought in a lot of noise for the sales team because the net was cast too wide.

* LinkedIn targeting almost certainly needs to be tightened up, both in terms of account priority and number of people targeted.  
  * Platform metrics (CTR, CPA, CPC) are suspiciously good for such a broad audience, so either the conversion definition is inflated or the audience expansion is cherry-picking easy converters.  
  * The 2-week segmented report suggests LinkedIn loaded up on easy converters early, but now the campaign is stale.  
* Google performance looks satisfactory on its face, but:  
  * I’d need to see a brand vs non-brand MQL breakdown.  
  * The low CPC implies that the average may be brought down by branded keyword spend. In that case, I would expect a higher CTR, so potentially something off there.  
* Third-party syndication was likely given too much budget relative to the campaign’s objective.  
  * Most, if not all, of the leads from here are spam at worst and unqualified at best.  
  * But, that MQL number does suggest that there’s at least some potential across these sources.

## Action Items

### QUICK WINS

1. Turn off LinkedIn Audience Network, Google Display Network, and Google auto-apply recommendations setting  
2. Divest from Third Party Syndication greatly, and go all in on the Syndication outlets that give us the most control and/or visibility into where our ads are being served.  
3. Reel in Google branded keyword spend, IF we have strong SEO brand protection.  
4. Trim LinkedIn account list, job function, and job title filters to get to a TAL of 25,000 at most.

### 30-DAY PROJECTS

* Scale up LinkedIn creative, Google RSA testing (Claude, LinkedIn/Google API)  
  * Actionable ad copy language with a clear value prop  
* First party data collection:   
  * Reverse IP lookup  
  * Google offline conversion import  
  * UTM structure verification  
  * Syndication vendor account filtering, if possible  
* Test LinkedIn LGFs vs landing pages  
  * I favor LGFs here, if you’re confident in your account list.  
* Confirm Google signal clarity  
  * Budget controls based on high intent keywords vs broader/educational searches

### TESTS TO CONSIDER

* Meta Ads, using some of the Third Party Syndicate budget  
  * Lower CPMs than LinkedIn with improving B2B targeting via custom audience uploads  
  * Scaled ad creative testing pivotal for this channel  
* Streaming TV, digital billboards for known, high profile decision makers  
  * MNTN, Basis, and LinkedIn's CTV partnerships  
  * Budget-intensive, so scope it as a small pilot against 50-100 named accounts.  
* LinkedIn thought leader ads  
  * About 55% of enterprise B2B organizations run them  
  * KNIME clearly has the subject matter expertise handy  
* LinkedIn Conversation Ads  
  * I’m dubious of their effectiveness, but some organizations have success with them.  
* Performance Max & Demand Gen Google campaigns, if sufficient budget and ad creative are available  
  * Performance Max for retargeting  
  * Demand Gen for YouTube  
  * Tight leashes on targeting and budget for both

## Communication Strategies

### MARKETING

* Overall Message: The demand is clearly there. We just need to tighten some areas up and verify signal clarity.  
* Create a project roadmap visualization, then break that roadmap into tasks in Asana for visibility and dependencies  
* Propose a bi-weekly sync between Marketing and Sales ops to review it together rather than in separate reporting silos.

### SALES

* Overall Message: Ensure alignment on lead quality as a primary performance metric, and get individual unqualified lead examples.  
* Highlight campaign segments with genuine high performance. This isn’t done as a diversion, but rather as strategic takeaways.  
* Make it collaborative rather than defensive by scheduling a lead scoring calibration session.
