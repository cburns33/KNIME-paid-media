Key Assumptions

  1. ACV is roughly $100K, so ~150 SALs are required over 12 months to hit the $15M pipeline target.
  2. At a 15% SAL rate from qualified leads, the program needs ~1,000 high-quality leads rather than raw form fills.
  3. Sales cycle runs 3 to 6 months. Evaluate on a cohort basis, not last-click. Month 1 and Month 2 reflect build-out costs. Efficiency compounds from Month 3 onward.
  1. KNIME has existing CRM data and open-source user registrants at manufacturing accounts to seed Phase 1 matched-list targeting. If unavailable, the Phase 1 timeline extends and cold-acquisition budget increases.
  2. MQL volume is a leading indicator only. Success is measured on SALs, pipeline contribution, and CAC efficiency.
  3. This program will target the North America region only.
Chase Burns · KNIME Paid Media Manager
  • $15M/~$100K ACV = 150 customers needed. At a 15% SAL rate = 1,000 qualified leads needed from $600K annual spend = $600 CPL target and $4K CPA per SAL ceiling.
  • SALs > MQLs: "I've seen programs that hit MQL targets and still got cut during restructuring because Sales didn't trust or didn't like the leads."

01 · Leading Indicators

Weeks 1 – 8 Signal quality

  • Micro-Conversion RateBy content type: templates vs. demo requests vs. pricing page visits.
  • Branded Search LiftWeek-over-week KNIME branded query volume growth.

02 · Program Health

Months 2 – 4 Marketing ↔ Sales

  • SAL Rate% of leads accepted by Sales. Primary Marketing and Sales feedback loop.
  • Cost per SALBy channel. Informs reallocation decisions at Circle 2 transition.
  • Pipeline Contribution$ influenced pipeline via cohort analysis, not last-click.
  • Circle Transition VelocityTime from first paid touchpoint to SAL.
03 · Business Impact

Months 6+ Executive reporting

  • Blended CACTotal spend ÷ new customers acquired. Reported alongside ICP match rate.
  • CAC : LTV ProxyBlended CAC vs. first-year ACV. Leading proxy: Business Hub credit utilization at 90 days post-acquisition.
  • Assisted ConversionsPaid touchpoints appearing in multi-touch closed-won paths.

Explicitly Excluded

  • MQL volume. Leading indicator only, never a success metric.
  • Impressions and reach. Reported, never optimized toward.
  • CTR as a performance signal. Conversion quality supersedes click volume.

Stakeholder Reporting

  • SalesWeekly. SAL rate, ICP match rate, pipeline contribution.
  • MarketingMonthly. Full Tier 1 to 3 dashboard, channel efficiency, circle transition progress.
  • ExecutiveQuarterly. Blended CAC, CAC : LTV proxy, pipeline vs. $15M target.
Chase Burns · KNIME Paid Media Manager
  • Optimize for the metric that is most closely aligned with value, not the one that's easiest to hit.
  • SAL rate is the handshake between Marketing and Sales. If SAL rate is low, we have a quality problem. If it is high but pipeline is not growing, we have a volume problem.
  • The CAC-to-LTV proxy using Business Hub credit utilization is KNIME-specific. It uses their own usage-based model as a leading signal for expansion revenue, which is only possible because I understand how their pricing model works.
