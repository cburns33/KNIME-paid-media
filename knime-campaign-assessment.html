Section 01
Data Gaps & Assumptions
Spend Reconciliation
The numbers don't add up.

It’s difficult to draw reliable conclusions without full spend reconciliation, but I proceeded assuming that the impression numbers should be higher.

Channel-Level Demo Rate
30% cumulative, but per channel?

The 30% cumulative demo-from-target-accounts figure is useful, but it would be helpful to know what that percentage looks like broken out by channel.

SALs & Conversion Value
Missing downstream metrics

Adequate detail is given to conclude that we’d definitely like for the campaign to perform better, but knowing if we did manage to get some SALs out of it would inform on the sales team’s temperature.

MQL Weighting
Are MQL weights channel-agnostic?

For this exercise's purposes, I've proceeded assuming they are for now, but in practice this is an assumption worth challenging, since LinkedIn and syndication leads tend to differ in signal quality.

Section 02
Performance Takeaways

Overall, these numbers look like they came from a primary objective of cost per MQL, but the campaign brought in a lot of noise for the sales team because the net was cast too wide.

Channel 1
LinkedIn
  • Targeting almost certainly needs to be tightened up, both in terms of account priority and number of people targeted.
  • Platform metrics (CTR, CPA, CPC) are suspiciously good for such a broad audience. Either the conversion definition is inflated or audience expansion is cherry-picking easy converters.
  • The 2-week segmented report suggests LinkedIn loaded up on easy converters early, but now the campaign is stale.
Channel 2
Google Search
  • Performance looks satisfactory on its face, but a brand vs. non-brand MQL breakdown is needed before drawing conclusions.
  • The low CPC implies the average may be brought down by branded keyword spend. In that case, a higher CTR would be expected, so something may be off there.
Channel 3
Third-Party Syndication
  • Likely given too much budget relative to the campaign's objective.
  • Most, if not all, of the leads from here are spam at worst and unqualified at best.
  • That MQL number does suggest there's at least some potential across these sources worth preserving at a reduced scale.
Section 03
Action Items
Quick Wins
1

Turn off LinkedIn Audience Network, Google Display Network, and Google auto-apply recommendations setting.

2

Divest from Third-Party Syndication greatly, and go all in on the syndication outlets that give the most control and/or visibility into where ads are being served.

3

Reel in Google branded keyword spend, if there is strong SEO brand protection in place.

4

Trim LinkedIn account list, job function, and job title filters to get to a TAL of 25,000 at most.

30-Day Projects
1

Scale up LinkedIn creative, Google RSA testing (Claude, LinkedIn/Google API)

  • Actionable ad copy language with a clear value prop
2

First-party data collection

  • Reverse IP lookup, Google offline conversion import, UTM structure verification, and Syndication vendor account filtering, if possible
3

Test LinkedIn LGFs vs. landing pages

  • LGFs preferred here, if confident in the account list
4

Confirm Google signal clarity

  • Budget controls based on high-intent keywords vs. broader/educational searches
Tests to Consider
1

Meta Ads, using some of the Third-Party Syndicate budget

  • Lower CPMs than LinkedIn with improving B2B targeting via custom audience uploads
  • Scaled ad creative testing pivotal for this channel
2

Streaming TV / digital billboards for known, high-profile decision makers

  • MNTN, Basis, and LinkedIn's CTV partnerships
  • Budget-intensive, so scope as a small pilot against 50–100 named accounts
3

LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads

  • ~55% of enterprise B2B organizations run them
  • KNIME clearly has the subject matter expertise
4

LinkedIn Conversation Ads

  • Dubious effectiveness, but some organizations have had success
5

Performance Max & Demand Gen Google campaigns, if sufficient budget and creative are available

  • Performance Max for retargeting; Demand Gen for YouTube
  • Tight leashes on targeting and budget for both
Section 04
Communication Strategy
Marketing
"The demand is clearly there. We just need to tighten some areas up and verify signal clarity."
  • Create a project roadmap visualization, then break it into tasks in Asana for visibility and dependencies.
  • Propose a bi-weekly sync between Marketing and Sales Ops to review it together rather than in separate reporting silos.
Sales
"Ensure alignment on lead quality as a primary performance metric, and get individual unqualified lead examples."
  • Highlight campaign segments with genuine high performance not as a diversion, but as strategic takeaways.
  • Make it collaborative rather than defensive by scheduling a lead scoring calibration session.
